After working 10 years in the music industry as a Sound Engineer and DJ, I felt the desire to switch career early 2017.
I remembered that, as a kid and teenager, I loved to created small scripts on my Atari STE or even hack some games. I just didn't know if I had what it takes to become a professional developper one day.

Then I discovered the impressive quantity and quality of online contents that were designed to teach people like me how to code.
I asked for advices and I have been told to give Python a try.

I quickly completed a first course created by MIT, Introduction to Computer Science and Python, and obtained a passing grade.
This course really got me hooked on coding and learning.

My first personal projects were driven by the idea of making my current job easier. Organizing my music files in my laptop or reading through tons of blog articles and charts to collect good tracks to play could be very boring and repetitive, this is something coding could definitely help with.
For several years alreadym I have been building websites with Wordpress, but now that I knew how to code, I wanted to know how to build a website from scratch.

Well, it happens Python is good at that too! I was already familiar with HTML and CSS, so I got into Django to learn to create the back-end.
My first project was to created a receipts organizer for my corporation, to scan the receipts and keep them organized in a database. I still have to bring some improvments to it (i-e scaning from a phone, design, security...) but anybody can sign up for an account and import documents scans already.

Right after that, I decided to learn Javascript, following the University of Pennsylvania online course, as I believe it is essential to Web Development.

Django is amazing but it's a bit too much boilerplate for very small apps like this one, so I learned Flask.

These days, I continue to dive deeper into Django, while extending my Front-End knowledge with Javascript Frameworks. As well, I am getting better at deploying my apps on a linux server, as Web apps belong to the Web !
